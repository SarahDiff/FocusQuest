THE TWO-ACCENT RULE — TEAL vs GOLD Each accent owns a distinct domain and they never swap. Teal (#5ec4c0) = the UI world — navigation, CTAs, active states, focus mode chrome, interactive elements. Gold (#d4a84b) = earned progress — XP bars, the timer ring, session log XP labels, level-up moments. The logic is intentional: teal is the realm you move through, gold is the treasure you accumulate. Together against the cool blue-black they read as moonlight + firelight — ethereal but warm at the point of achievement.

SURFACE TINT SHIFT FROM V1 In v1, surfaces had a warm green tint. Here every surface leans cool blue — rgba(16,22,32) rather than rgba(22,30,24). This is subtle but critical: it means your painted background (whatever cool moonlit scene you create in Figma) will feel continuous with the UI rather than fighting it. The character card's teal top-border glow is new — it acts as a horizon line, like moonlight cresting over a ridge.

WHY GOLD BARS WORK HERE Against the cool blue-black, gold reads as alchemical warmth — like a vein of ore running through stone, or candlelight glimpsed through a window. The glow (box-shadow: 0 0 10px rgba(212,168,75,0.22)) is stronger than v2's teal bars, because gold against cool blue has higher inherent contrast. The shimmer feels like the bar is genuinely lit from within.

TEAL ICON USAGE Active nav items, XP labels, and the primary sigil (◈) can be rendered in teal with a subtle text-shadow glow. All other icons remain in var(--text-muted) unless actively selected. This keeps the magical quality of teal reserved for things that matter — it should feel like something glowing, not a standard icon colour.
08
Voice & Copy — Unchanged from v1
✓ Do
"Your time. Your legend."
"The realm stirs as you work."
"Each session a step. Each hour a stone laid on the path only you will walk."
"Stay in the moment"
"More paths unlock as you grow"
New in v2: ambient copy can use the moonbeam colour (var(--moon)) to add an extra register — softer, more environmental, like the world speaking rather than the UI.
✕ Avoid
"You missed 3 days in a row!"
"Complete your daily goal"
"Streak broken 😢"
"You're doing great! 🎉"
No guilt, no exclamation marks, no emoji in functional UI copy. The voice is always the world speaking to you, not an app demanding your attention.
